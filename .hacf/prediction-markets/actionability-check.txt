Beginning state:
No tooling exists to safely test prediction market strategies. To experiment with Kalshi, Polymarket, Manifold, or PredictIt strategies, real money would need to be risked or trades manually simulated in a spreadsheet, with no structured way to measure performance or replay historical markets.

Transformations:
1. Historical market data from all four platforms is ingested and stored locally
2. A paper trading engine simulates order execution against that data without touching real funds
3. Pluggable strategy modules (Kelly sizing, arb scanner, closing momentum, news spike fade, market maker) can be attached and run
4. A backtester replays strategies against historical markets and produces scored results
5. A portfolio tracker computes P&L, Brier score, Sharpe ratio, and win rate in real time and historically

End states (observable success conditions):
- The application can be opened, a strategy toggled on, and paper trades execute against live or historical market data
- A backtest runs and returns scored results (P&L curve, Sharpe ratio, win rate, Brier score) without any real money changing hands
- Strategy performance can be compared across platforms and time ranges from a single local dashboard
